Design Decisions & Heuristics

Recognition over Recall (Nielsen): Each template uses a consistent layout pattern (eyebrow tag → headline → supporting details → implicit CTA) so followers recognize the format within 0.5s and know what to expect.

Von Restorff Effect: The accent color (#5e6ad2) is used sparingly — headlines, key numbers, arrows. On the dark canvas it's the distinctive element that draws attention to the main message.

Fitts's Law: Call-to-action elements (logos, arrows, "Start free" buttons) are positioned at the bottom — the natural thumb zone for mobile scrolling and the endpoint of F-pattern scanning.

Hick's Law / Choice Overload: Per-slide content is limited to 1 main idea + 3–4 supporting points. No slide asks the viewer to process more than one headline and a short list.

Aesthetic-Usability Effect: Dark moody backgrounds with precise spacing (8px grid) and consistent 1px borders signal polish and trust — important for a tool that handles career documents.

WCAG Contrast: Navy (#1E3A5F) on dark bg meets contrast. Text at #f7f8f8 on #010102 = 18.1:1 ratio. Accent #5e6ad2 on dark = 5.3:1 (AA large text, meets AAA for body on light bg variations).

Inverted Pyramid: Every slide leads with the boldest claim in the headline, then supports with secondary copy. The "so what" is always above the fold.

Pareto (80/20): 80% of the visual impact comes from the headline + accent color. Background, borders, and muted copy recede so the value prop hits fast in a busy feed.

Progressive Disclosure: Carousel covers tease the insight without giving everything away — the "swipe" promise is that slides 2–n deliver the full value.
